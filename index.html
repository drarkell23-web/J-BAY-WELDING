I. Metallurgy & Corrosion Control

In coastal regions like J-Bay, "Tea Staining" on stainless steel is a common failure. This occurs when the passive layer of the steel is compromised. We recommend Electro-Polishing or Passivation for all sea-front installations to ensure longevity.

TIP: Grade 304 (18% Cr, 8% Ni) vs Grade 316 (16% Cr, 10% Ni, 2% Mo). The Molybdenum in 316 is what stops the sea air from eating your metal.

II. The Load-Bearing Standard

Structural integrity isn't just about the thickness of the beam; it's about the Heat Affected Zone (HAZ) of the weld. Excessive heat during welding can "anneal" the surrounding steel, making it brittle. Our coded welders use multi-pass techniques to manage thermal input.

Quick Maintenance Checklist

01

Inspect welds for hairline fractures or "crevice corrosion" every 12 months in coastal zones.

02

Ensure Hot-Dip Galvanizing meets SANS 121 / ISO 1461 thickness requirements (min 85 microns).

03

Wash stainless steel structures with fresh water monthly to remove salt crystal buildup.
